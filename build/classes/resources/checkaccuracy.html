Check peptide accuracy

MS Reader has a feature to check the accuracy of peptides within your sample. Every Auto HDX run will automatically compare the isotopic distribution of your peak to the theoretical isotopic envelope and derive an accuracy percentage, displayed on the HDX form. You can also check the accuracy of a peptide before running Auto HDX. From the file menu, select "Check peptide accuracy", with your non-deuterated sample loaded. MS Reader will extract your peptide and score it against the theoretical distribution. Typically, scores above 75% can be considered good matches. However, sequencing cannot be done by a single MS instrument - you must first compare tandem MS data to get accurate sequences. MS Reader only helps to verify you are seeing the same peptide each time.
